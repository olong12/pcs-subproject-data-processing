              SteinbergFebruary 23, 1999An act to add Section 22825.18 to, and to add and repeal Article 4 (commencing with Section 19819.9) of Chapter 1 of Part 2.6 of Division 5 of Title 2 of, the Government Code, relating to state employment.LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTAB 688, Steinberg.
                          State employment:  health benefits.(1) Existing law generally sets forth the duties of the Department of Personnel Administration with respect to the administration of certain employee entitlements.This bill would establish the Health Care Quality and Cost Protection Committee to explore and develop innovative health benefit cost containment concepts.  It would require the committee to report and recommend specific cost containment findings to the Legislature and the Board of Administration of the Public Employees’ Retirement System by January 1, 2002.This bill would repeal these provisions as of January 1, 2003.(2) Existing law, the Public Employees’ Medical and Hospital Care Act, provides for health benefit plans and contracts for state employees and sets forth the employer’s contributions to those plans, as specified.This bill would require the employer’s contribution to include any increases in health benefits plan premiums for each excluded employee, as defined, and for each state employee, as defined, payable from January 1, 2001, to July 2, 2001, inclusive.Digest KeyBill TextThe people of the State of California do enact as follows:SECTION 1. Article 4 (commencing with Section 19819.9) is added to Chapter 1 of Part 2.6 of Division 5 of Title 2 of the Government Code, to read:
                Article
               4. Health Care Quality and Cost Protection Committee19819.9. It is the intent of the Legislature to ensure that the interests of state employees are protected in the provision of quality health care services.  In furtherance of this goal, it is essential that the state employer and employee organizations work cooperatively to identify and implement health plans and health plan options that provide quality health care while containing escalating medical and pharmaceutical costs.19819.91. (a) The Health Care Quality and Cost Protection Committee is hereby established to be supported by the Department of Personnel Administration.(b) The committee shall explore and develop innovative health benefit cost containment concepts.  The committee membership shall be determined through collective bargaining agreements between the state and the exclusive representatives for state employees.19819.92 The committee established pursuant to this article shall report and recommend specific cost containment findings to the Legislature and the Board of Administration of the Public Employees’ Retirement System by January 1, 2002.19819.95. This article shall become inoperative on July 1, 2002, and, as of January 1, 2003, is repealed, unless a later enacted statute that is enacted before January 1, 2003, deletes or extends the dates on which it becomes inoperative and is repealed.SEC. 2. Section 22825.18 is added to the Government Code, to read:22825.18. Notwithstanding Section 22825, Section 22825.1, or any other provision of law, the employer’s contribution with respect to each excluded employee, as defined by subdivision (b) of Section 3527, who is otherwise eligible, and each state employee, as defined in subdivision (c) of Section 3513, who is otherwise eligible, shall include all increases in health benefits plan premiums payable from January 1, 2001, to July 2, 2001, inclusive.
