                          Special interest license plates:  California history.Existing law requires the Department of Motor Vehicles to issue special interest license plates, as specified, containing a distinct design or decal of participating organizations, to any person, to be displayed in lieu of regular license plates.This bill would add a special interest license plate that bears a distinctive design or decal for each county to the special interest license plate program.  The bill would require the additional fees collected by the issuance, renewal, transfer, and substitution of these special interest license plates, to be deposited in the Bicentennial Fund, created by the bill.  The bill would require that, upon appropriation, 1/2 of the money in the fund be used by the Secretary of State for purposes of preserving California history and 1/2be used as grants to local governments or nonprofit organizations to be deposited in the California Heritage Fund for  described state historical preservation purposes.The bill would also require any organization participating in a special license plate program pursuant to the bill to comply with all of the statutory requirements imposed generally on organizations participating in special interest license plate programs.Digest Key

      NO  Bill TextThe people of the State of California do enact as follows:SECTION 1. Section 5081 is added to the Vehicle Code, to read:5081. (a) The department, in consultation with the  Secretary of State, shall design and make available for issuance pursuant to this article special interest license plates.  Notwithstanding Section 5060, these plates shall bear a plates.  These license plates shall bear a distinctive design or decal for each county.  Any person described in Section 5101, upon payment of the additional fees set forth in subdivision (b), may apply for and be issued a set of these special interest license plates for the particular county of the person’s residence.  Notwithstanding subdivision (a) of Section 5060, the plates may be issued in a combination of numbers or letters, or both, requested by the owner or lessee of the vehicle.(b) In addition to the regular fees for an original registration or renewal of registration and subject to a fee adjustment under subdivision (c), the following additional fees shall be paid for the issuance, renewal, or transfer of the special interest license plates authorized pursuant to this section:(1) For the original issuance of the plates, fifty dollars ($50).(2) For a renewal of registration with the plates, forty dollars ($40).(3) For transfer of the plates to another vehicle, fifteen dollars ($15).(4) For each substitute replacement plate, thirty-five dollars ($35). (5) For the conversion of an existing special interest or environmental license plate to the special interest license plate authorized pursuant to this section, sixty-five dollars ($65).(c) After deducting its administrative costs under this section, the department shall deposit the additional revenues derived from the issuance, renewal, transfer, and substitution of special environmental design license plates in the Bicentennial Fund, which is hereby created.  Upon appropriation by the Legislature, the money in the fund shall be allocated by the Controller as follows:(1) One-half of those funds to the Secretary of State for purposes of preserving California history.(2)One-half of those funds as grants to local governments or nonprofit organizations that are located in those counties in which persons have paid additional fees for the special interest license plates under this section.  Funds shall be allocated under this paragraph to local governments or nonprofit organizations in a county proportional to the amount of additional revenues received under this section from persons in that particular county to the total amount of additional revenues received statewide.  Grants shall be awarded to those local governmental entities or nonprofit organizations that provide state historical preservation programs and services and have received the approval of the Secretary of State.  Funds that are apportioned to the local governmental entities or nonprofit organizations shall be used for state historical preservation purposes only.(d)Except as specifically provided in this section, the requirements of Section 5060 shall apply.(2) One-half of those funds shall be deposited in the California Heritage Fund, established pursuant to Section 5079.10 of the Public Resources Code.  Programs funded by the State Office of Historic Preservation from funds deposited pursuant to this paragraph, shall be disbursed pursuant to grants, loans, or contracts that involve public or private entities that are located in those counties in which persons have paid additional fees for the special interest license plates under this section.  Funds shall be allocated under this paragraph to those cities in a county proportional to the amount of additional revenues received under this section from persons in that particular county to the total amount of additional revenues received statewide.  Funds shall be disbursed to those entities that support the management, acquisition, preservation, rehabilitation, restoration, and adaptive use of state historical properties.  Funds described in this paragraph shall be used for state historical preservation purposes described in this paragraph.(d) Any organization participating in the special interest license plate program pursuant to this section shall comply with all of the requirements imposed on participating organizations pursuant to Section 5060.
